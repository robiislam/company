Back to Basics: What Is Virtualization All About?

Service virtualization is a sophisticated technique that enables organizations to simulate and efficiently manage test environments, thus bringing quality software to the market faster and at a lower cost. In simple words, service virtualization is a computer-generated simulation of a software product with all necessary elements integrated. It's also worth mentioning that the original product may be under development or in a live deployment.

When it comes to virtualization, you better understand the difference between server and service virtualization. First one sets up only the server virtually. With server virtualization, it becomes way more comfortable to study the behavior of connected elements and components in a demo version than in a live environment.

While the second one, service virtualization, is all about replicating any interaction between the product and the server. Service virtualization creates live copies of original product deployment scenarios, making it easier for QA teams to identify failures and issues that would have occurred when the product goes live for users.
